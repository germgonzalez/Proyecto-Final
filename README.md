# Proyecto-Final

Integrantes:
Germán González
Alberto Daniel Ayala Cabrera
Javier Beloso
Elías Fernández Basti

This final electronic engineering project describes the development of an Intelligent Remote Management Node for LED streetlights up to 300W, designed to operate under the electrical grid conditions of Argentina and comply with the international NEMA 7 standard. The device integrates a power stage with a switched-mode power supply, transient protection, and a system for measuring electrical parameters (voltage, current, and power factor) for efficient energy management. Communication is established via GPRS technology, allowing remote control of luminous flux through the 1-10V protocol and real-time monitoring from a centralized management platform (dashboard). The system not only enables the automation of dimming profiles and fault reporting but also proposes a domestically manufactured alternative with competitive costs compared to imported products. The project results demonstrate a significant improvement in operational efficiency, a reduction in maintenance costs, and effective integration into the Smart City ecosystem.
